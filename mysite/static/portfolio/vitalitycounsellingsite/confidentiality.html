Confidentiality

All information provided in counselling is held in the utmost confidence. All notes, records and communications created concerning your involvement as a client are kept secure and can only be shared with your written consent.

There are three exceptions to confidentiality when the law requires that clinical records be disclosed with or without written consent. These strict conditions are:

  1. If the counsellor has reason to believe that a child (a person under the age of 12) is at risk of being abused, neglected or abandoned by their parents or another caregiver, Family and Childrenâ€™s Services must be notified.
  2. If the counsellor believes that there is an imminent risk of suicide or homicide, the police must be called.
  3. If a court order or subpoena is received by the counsellor to release a file to the court.

At the beginning of the first session, the topic of Confidentiality will be discussed. You are encouraged to ask any questions about these exceptions with me prior to discussing your situation.
